# Virginia EMS Scope of Practice: BLS, ALS, and CCT/Supplemented Transport

## Governing Documents

1. **Code of Virginia, Title 32.1, Chapter 5.1 (Article 5.1 EMS)** — § 32.1-111.1 et seq. (definitions), § 32.1-111.6 (licensing)
2. **12VAC5-31 — Virginia Emergency Medical Services Regulations** — primary regulatory framework
3. **EMS Procedures and Medications Schedule** — Board of Health approved
4. **EMS Scope of Practice Formulary** — medications by certification level (OEMS)
5. **EMS Scope of Practice Procedures** — procedures by certification level (OEMS)

All via Virginia Office of Emergency Medical Services (OEMS): <https://www.vdh.virginia.gov/emergency-medical-services/>

---

## BLS (Basic Life Support)

### Definition (12VAC5-31-10)
Care by personnel certified as **First Responder**, **EMR**, or **EMT** (or Board-equivalent).

### Staffing (12VAC5-31-1230, 12VAC5-31-1240)
- **Min 2 persons**: operator (license + EVOC) + AIC (must be EMT-certified)
- BLS air medical: still requires at minimum a VT-certified Paramedic on crew (fixed wing excluded)

### Certification Levels (12VAC5-31-1305/1307/1315)
- EMS First Responder (FR), Emergency Medical Responder (EMR), Emergency Medical Technician (EMT)

---

## ALS (Advanced Life Support)

### Definition (12VAC5-31-10)
Care by personnel certified as **EMT-Enhanced**, **AEMT**, **Intermediate**, or **Paramedic**. Air ALS = invasive procedures + medication/IV administration.

### Staffing (12VAC5-31-1250)
1. Vehicle with ALS equipment package
2. AIC: ALS-level certified provider
3. Attendant: at minimum EMT-certified (can be the operator if EMT); cannot serve as AIC simultaneously

### Certification Levels (12VAC5-31-1325/1335/1337/1345)
- EMT-E, AEMT, Intermediate, Paramedic

## CCT / Supplemented Transport

### Virginia Does NOT Have a Separate "CCT" Classification
CCT-equivalent services handled as **Supplemented Transport** (12VAC5-31-1260).

### Supplemented Transport (12VAC5-31-1260)
**Prerequisites:**
- ALS-equipped ambulance AND sending physician determines patient care exceeds ALS personnel scope OR equipment needs exceed ALS equipment package

**Staffing:**
- AIC: **Physician, RN, or PA** trained/experienced in the specific care & equipment
- Attendant: EMT-certified (third person, not the operator)
- Operator: license + EVOC

**Agency duties:**
- Written statement from sending physician (condition + equipment)
- Verify AIC competence
- Must refuse transport if requirements not met — document refusal

### Neonatal Transport (12VAC5-31-1270)
- Min 3 persons (operator + AIC + attendant, one must be EMT)
- AIC: Physician; or RN/PA licensed 2+ yrs with specialized neonatal training; or OEMS-approved equivalent

### Air Medical Critical Care (12VAC5-31-10)
Interfacility transport of critically ill/injured patient requiring care at **physician or RN** scope level.

## Scope of Practice Governance (12VAC5-31-1050)
All personnel perform only procedures for which certified, within:
1. Local medical treatment protocols
2. OMD medical direction  
3. EMS Procedures and Medications Schedule (Board approved)

Related: 12VAC5-31-1070 (extraordinary care), 12VAC5-31-1080 (medical control orders), 12VAC5-31-1090 (refusal of care), 12VAC5-31-1100 (consent)
