# Virginia EMS Scope of Practice: BLS, ALS, and CCT/Supplemented Transport

## Governing Documents

The scope of practice for BLS, ALS, and critical care transport in Virginia is governed by:

1. **Code of Virginia, Title 32.1, Chapter 5.1 (Article 5.1 EMS)** — particularly § 32.1-111.1 et seq. (definitions) and § 32.1-111.6 (licensing authority)
2. **Virginia Administrative Code, 12VAC5-31 — Virginia Emergency Medical Services Regulations** — the primary regulatory framework
3. **Virginia EMS Procedures and Medications Schedule** — approved by the Board of Health, defines specific procedures and medications by certification level
4. **Virginia EMS Scope of Practice Formulary** — the drug formulary by certification level (maintained by OEMS)
5. **Virginia EMS Scope of Practice Procedures** — procedural authorizations by certification level (maintained by OEMS)
6. **OEMS Medical Direction Committee** — develops and updates the scope of practice documents

All sources accessible via the Virginia Office of Emergency Medical Services (OEMS), part of the Virginia Department of Health: <https://www.vdh.virginia.gov/emergency-medical-services/>

## 1. Basic Life Support (BLS)

### Regulatory Definition (12VAC5-31-10)
BLS = care provided by EMS personnel certified as **First Responder**, **Emergency Medical Responder (EMR)**, or **Emergency Medical Technician (EMT)**, or equivalent as approved by the Board of Health.

### Agency Licensure (12VAC5-31-410)
EMS agencies may be licensed for:
- Nontransport first response: **Basic life support**
- Ground ambulance: **Basic life support**

### Staffing Requirements (12VAC5-31-1230, 12VAC5-31-1240)
- **Minimum 2 persons** on any ground ambulance transport:
  - **Operator**: Valid driver's license + approved EVOC (Emergency Vehicle Operator Course)
  - **Attendant-in-charge (AIC)**: Must be certified as **EMT** (or equivalent approved by OEMS)

### Certification Levels within BLS (12VAC5-31-1305/1307/1315)
- **EMS First Responder** (FR)
- **Emergency Medical Responder** (EMR)
- **Emergency Medical Technician** (EMT) — only EMT can serve as AIC during BLS transport

### Scope of Practice (12VAC5-31-1050)
BLS providers perform only procedures, skills, or techniques for which certified, acting within:
- Local medical treatment protocols
- Medical direction of the Operational Medical Director (OMD)
- The EMS Procedures and Medications Schedule

### Air Medical Environment
In air transport, BLS-level care is defined as care commensurate with EMT scope of practice. Virginia requires that air medical crews include at minimum a VT-certified **Paramedic** (fixed wing excluded).

## 2. Advanced Life Support (ALS)

### Regulatory Definition (12VAC5-31-10)
ALS = care provided by EMS personnel certified as **EMT-Enhanced**, **Advanced EMT (AEMT)**, **Intermediate**, or **Paramedic**, or equivalent as approved by the Board of Health.

ALS in the air medical environment = transport of a patient receiving care including **invasive medical procedures** or **administration of medications** (including IV infusions), in addition to authorized noninvasive care.

### Agency Licensure (12VAC5-31-410)
EMS agencies may be licensed for:
- Nontransport first response: **Advanced life support**
- Ground ambulance: **Advanced life support**

### Staffing Requirements (12VAC5-31-1250)
During ALS transport:
1. **Vehicle**: Ground ambulance equipped with ALS equipment package
2. **Attendant-in-charge**: Must be certified as an **ALS-level provider** (AEMT, Intermediate, or Paramedic)
3. **Attendant**: Must be certified as at least **EMT** (or equivalent) — cannot serve as AIC simultaneously; the operator may serve as the attendant if EMT-certified

### Certification Levels within ALS (12VAC5-31-1325/1335/1337/1345)
- **EMT-Enhanced** (EMT-E)
- **Advanced EMT** (AEMT)
- **Intermediate**
- **Paramedic**

Bridge courses exist: AEMT→Intermediate, EMT-E→Intermediate, Intermediate→Paramedic, RN→Paramedic

### Scope of Practice (12VAC5-31-1050)
Same governance framework as BLS — certified level + local protocols + OMD authorization + Procedures and Medications Schedule. Key differentiator: ALS providers may perform **invasive procedures** and **administer medications** including IV therapy.

## 3. Critical Care Transport (CCT) / Supplemented Transport

### Important: Virginia Does Not Have a Separate "CCT" Certification
Virginia's regulatory framework does **not** define a separate "Critical Care Transport" (CCT) certification or ambulance classification. Instead, CCT-equivalent services are handled under **"Supplemented Transport"** (12VAC5-31-1260).

### Supplemented Transport Requirements (12VAC5-31-1260)

**A. Prerequisites (one of the following must be true):**
1. Ambulance equipped with ALS intermediate/paramedic equipment package; AND
2. Sending physician determines the patient's medically necessary care **exceeds the scope** of available ALS-certified personnel; OR
3. Sending physician determines the equipment needed **exceeds** what is required for ALS AEMT/Intermediate/Paramedic ground ambulance equipment

**B. Staffing:**
1. **Attendant-in-charge**: Must be a **physician, registered nurse (RN), or physician assistant (PA)** who is trained and experienced in the care and equipment needed for the specific patient
2. **Attendant**: Must be certified as **EMT** (or equivalent) — must be a **third person** who is NOT the operator
3. **Operator**: Valid driver's license + EVOC

**C. Agency Responsibilities:**
- Obtain written statement from sending physician detailing the patient's medical condition and required equipment (may be documented as transport orders)
- Verify that the AIC is experienced in the patient care required and operation of all equipment
- **Must refuse** the transport if requirements cannot be satisfied — refusal must be documented

### Neonatal Transport (12VAC5-31-1270)
Separate from general CCT, neonatal transport has its own requirements:
- **Minimum 3 persons**: Operator + AIC + Attendant (one must be EMT-certified)
- **AIC must be**: Physician; or RN/PA licensed for 2+ years with specialized neonatal transport training; or equivalent as approved by OEMS
- Vehicle must have additional neonatal equipment per 12VAC5-31-860

### Air Medical Critical Care (12VAC5-31-10 Definition)
In the air medical environment, "Critical Care" = interfacility transport of critically ill/injured patient whose condition warrants care commensurate with the scope of practice of a **physician or registered nurse**.

## Scope of Practice Governance (12VAC5-31-1050)
All EMS personnel shall only perform procedures, skills, or techniques for which currently licensed/certified, provided they act in accordance with:
1. **Local medical treatment protocols**
2. **Medical direction of the OMD** of the licensed EMS agency
3. **EMS Procedures and Medications Schedule** approved by the Board of Health

Key related sections:
- **12VAC5-31-1070**: Extraordinary care outside of protocols
- **12VAC5-31-1080**: Inability to carry out medical control orders
- **12VAC5-31-1090**: Refusal of care
- **12VAC5-31-1100**: Consent or refusal
